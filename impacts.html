Impacts

Community affected
by The Typhoon

Over four million people are spread across 6,377 barangays and 419 municipalities in 58 provinces and 15 regions. Iloilo was the most affected province in terms of agriculture, with P612 million in damages, while Aklan was the most affected in terms of infrastructure, with P325 million in damages. Typhoon Frank caused P2,012,461,435.75 in total damage in Aklan, Philippines, with 43,068 houses partially destroyed and 11,210 completely destroyed, affecting 358,946 people.

The Regional Disaster Coordinating Council 6, Typhoon "Frank" damaged 3.2 billion in agricultural and fish products, 300 schools, 420,000 hectares of land, and 925,000 homes. The damages were mostly in infrastructure and agriculture, with Iloilo suffering the most in agriculture at P612 million and Aklan suffering the most in infrastructure at P325 million.



Community Preparation & Recovery

There were only a few warnings about heavy rain and flooding, but residents in some areas claimed they were unaware of the upcoming flood. They also discussed relocating informal settlers to a much safer area; however, there were no pre-evacuation stations because no one expected a relatively large amount of flooding due to rainfall, resulting in no preparations or precautionary measures. They recovered by sending responders from the local community and the LGU to evacuate people affected by the flash flood, and there were many donations both locally and internationally.







Geography

Region VI is prone to typhoons due to its location in the Philippines, which is frequently hit by tropical storms. The Philippines is one of the most cyclone-prone countries in the world, lying on what is often described as the ‘typhoon belt’. Approximately 19-20 cyclones enter the Philippine Area of Responsibility annually, with 7-9 reaching landfall.



Benefit of Typhoons

For some areas, if there are no typhoons, there will be no crop growth and agricultural harvest in these areas. Typhoons can sometimes play beneficial role in relieving drought. In addition, typhoons play an important role in regulating the earth’s heat and maintaining the heat balance.
